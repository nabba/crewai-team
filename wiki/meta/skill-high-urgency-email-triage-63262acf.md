**Skill: High-Urgency Email Triage**

**Topic**
Ranking and distilling high volumes of email to isolate critical actions.

**When to use**
Use when a user has a cluttered inbox and needs to identify the most urgent tasks, legal obligations, or direct outreach without scanning every message.

**Procedure**
1. **Aggregate:** Retrieve all emails from the specified timeframe (e.g., "yesterday").
2. **Categorize:** Sort messages into buckets: Legal/Contracts, Direct Personal Outreach, Financial/Organizational, and Low-Priority (Newsletters/Marketing).
3. **Rank:** Order the "High Priority" buckets by urgency (e.g., Docusign deadlines first, followed by direct follow-ups).
4. **Extract:** Summarize the core action required for each high-priority item.
5. **Present:** Deliver a numbered list of the top most important messages, explicitly separating them from the noise.

**Pitfalls**
*   **Over-filtering:** Accidentally categorizing a nuanced professional request as "marketing."
*   **Ignoring Temporal Clues:** Missing urgency if the filter only looks at keywords and not the date/time of the last reminder.
*   **Vague Summaries:** Listing the sender without listing the specific action required (e.g., "Email from Erin" vs "Sign C2PA Agreement").
